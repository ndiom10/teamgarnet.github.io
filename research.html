Research & Findings

Exploring how artificial intelligence impacts student learning, critical thinking, writing, and cognitive independence.

About This Study

Team Garnet at the University of Virginia conducted a meta-analysis investigating whether AI enhances education or gradually weakens core academic skills through overreliance.

Our work compares historical tools like calculators with modern AI systems like ChatGPT to understand how learning habits are changing.

Conclusion

AI is not inherently harmful. The danger comes when students replace learning with automation.

Used correctly, AI can increase access, tutoring, and efficiency. Used carelessly, it can erode the very skills education is meant to build.
