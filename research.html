Research

This study investigates whether artificial intelligence improves education or gradually weakens academic skills through overreliance.

Methodology

Our team reviewed historical learning technologies, modern AI studies, survey data, and peer-reviewed academic research. We compared earlier tools like calculators with modern systems such as ChatGPT to evaluate educational impact.

Key Findings

Cognitive Skills

Frequent AI dependence may reduce active reasoning, memory retention, and persistence through difficult tasks.

Writing & Language

Recent studies found increasing AI-influenced language patterns in academic and professional writing.

Educational Benefits

AI can provide tutoring support, personalized learning, accessibility tools, and teacher assistance.

Comparative Insight

Unlike calculators, AI performs higher-order tasks such as writing, summarizing, brainstorming, and reasoning. This creates major opportunities, but also higher risks of deskilling.

Conclusion

AI is most valuable when it supports learning rather than replacing struggle, reflection, and independent thought.
