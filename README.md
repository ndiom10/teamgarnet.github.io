# Team Garnet – AI and Deskilling in Education

University of Virginia  
CS 4501: AI & Humanity  

## Overview

This project explores how artificial intelligence is transforming education and whether heavy reliance on AI tools may contribute to **deskilling** among students.

As generative AI becomes common in classrooms, students now use these tools for writing, brainstorming, coding, studying, and solving problems. While AI offers clear benefits such as accessibility, tutoring support, and productivity, it may also reduce opportunities to build core academic skills through struggle, repetition, and independent thinking.

Our research investigates the balance between these benefits and risks.

## Main Research Questions

- Does AI reduce students’ critical thinking over time?
- Is writing quality improving or becoming dependent on automation?
- Are students offloading memory and reasoning to machines?
- Can AI be used as a learning assistant rather than a shortcut?
- How should universities respond to widespread AI adoption?
