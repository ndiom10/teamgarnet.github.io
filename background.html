Research

Our research uses a meta-study approach to examine how artificial intelligence affects student learning, writing, reasoning, creativity, and confidence. Instead of relying on one study, Team Garnet reviewed multiple academic sources, professional opinions, and historical comparisons to understand the broader pattern.

Methodology

Study Selection

We selected studies from different time periods, including research on calculators, internet use, and modern AI tools. This allowed us to compare older educational technologies with newer generative AI systems.

Analytical Approach

Our team looked for repeated themes across sources, including cognitive offloading, deskilling, skill erosion, educational benefits, and student dependence. We also considered limitations because AI in education is still new and long-term data remains limited.

Major Findings

Cognitive Skills

AI can reduce mental effort when students rely on it too quickly. This may weaken memory, confidence, and active learning habits over time.

Writing and Language

Research shows that AI tools are already influencing writing patterns, especially in academic and scientific writing. The concern is not only cheating, but also a decline in originality and language diversity.

Creativity

AI can support brainstorming, but it can also reduce the discomfort and effort that often produce original ideas. Human judgment remains necessary.

Critical Thinking

Overreliance on AI can lead students to accept answers without questioning them. This is risky because AI can hallucinate, simplify, or reflect bias.

Educational Benefits

AI also has clear benefits. It can act as a tutor, support students with fewer resources, help teachers plan lessons, and make learning more accessible when used responsibly.

Key Takeaway

Our research suggests that AI is not automatically harmful or automatically beneficial. The impact depends on how students use it. When AI supports learning, it can be powerful. When it replaces struggle, reasoning, and practice, it can contribute to deskilling.
