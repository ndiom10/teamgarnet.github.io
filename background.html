Research

This study investigates whether artificial intelligence strengthens education or gradually weakens core academic skills through overreliance.

Methodology

Our team reviewed historical educational technologies, modern AI studies, survey data, and academic research from multiple decades. We compared past tools like calculators with present AI systems such as ChatGPT to evaluate long-term educational impact.

Key Findings

Cognitive Skills

Frequent AI dependence may reduce active reasoning, memory retention, and persistence through difficult tasks.

Writing & Language

Recent studies found growing AI-influenced language patterns in academic and professional writing.

Educational Benefits

AI can provide tutoring support, personalized learning, accessibility tools, and teacher assistance.

Comparative Insight

Unlike calculators, AI performs higher-order cognitive tasks such as writing, summarizing, brainstorming, and reasoning. This creates greater opportunities for learning support, but also greater risk of deskilling when students rely on AI too heavily.

Conclusion

AI is most effective when it supports learning rather than replacing struggle, reflection, and independent thought.
